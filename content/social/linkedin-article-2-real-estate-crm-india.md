# The Real Estate Lead Problem in India — And Why Generic CRMs Make It Worse

*By Ankit Mehta, Founder — Khosha Systems*

---

An Indian real estate developer running a project in Pune receives leads from 99acres, MagicBricks, Housing.com, Facebook Ads, Google Ads, walk-ins, referrals, and channel partners.

On a good month, that's 500+ leads across 8+ sources.

On a typical month, 30–40% of those leads get no follow-up at all. Not because the sales team is lazy — because there's no system to ensure it happens.

This is the real estate lead problem in India, and it's costing developers crores in lost sales.

## Why Generic CRMs Fail Real Estate

Salesforce, HubSpot, and Zoho CRM are excellent products. They were built for SaaS companies, e-commerce businesses, and B2B sales teams selling monthly subscriptions or $50K enterprise deals.

Indian real estate sales have almost nothing in common with those workflows.

**The lead sources are different.** A SaaS company gets leads from a website form and maybe LinkedIn. A real estate developer gets leads from 5+ property portals, each with its own format, plus social ads, plus offline channels. A generic CRM requires manual CSV imports or expensive custom integrations for each portal.

**The sales cycle is different.** A SaaS deal might close in 2 weeks. A real estate deal takes 3–6 months, involves multiple site visits, family decision-makers, loan approvals, and negotiations. The CRM needs to manage a fundamentally different pipeline structure.

**The follow-up requirements are different.** In Indian real estate, WhatsApp is the primary communication channel — not email. A CRM that can't send automated WhatsApp messages, track WhatsApp conversations, and trigger follow-ups based on WhatsApp engagement is missing the primary channel.

**The compliance requirements are different.** RERA mandates specific documentation, disclosure timelines, and audit trails. A generic CRM has no concept of RERA compliance.

**The channel partner model is different.** Real estate developers work with hundreds of independent brokers and channel partners. These partners need their own portal — with inventory visibility, deal tracking, commission transparency, and lead submission. Generic CRMs don't have this.

## What Happens Without a Purpose-Built System

We've talked to dozens of real estate developers across India. The pattern is remarkably consistent:

**Stage 1: Excel and WhatsApp.** The sales team tracks leads in a shared Excel file and communicates via personal WhatsApp. Leads get duplicated across salespeople. Follow-ups are inconsistent. The developer has zero visibility into what's actually happening.

**Stage 2: Generic CRM (partial adoption).** The developer buys Zoho or a similar CRM. The sales team uses it for a few weeks, then drifts back to Excel because the CRM doesn't match their workflow. Portal leads still need manual entry. WhatsApp isn't integrated. Site visit tracking requires workarounds.

**Stage 3: Expensive custom setup.** The developer hires a consultant to customize the generic CRM with portal integrations, WhatsApp APIs, and custom pipeline stages. This costs 3–5x the CRM license and takes months. By the time it's done, the team has lost momentum and trust in the system.

**Stage 4: Back to square one.** The customized system is brittle, expensive to maintain, and doesn't scale to new projects. The cycle repeats.

## The Math Behind Lost Leads

Let's be specific about what this costs.

A developer with 500 leads per month and a 3% conversion rate closes 15 deals per month. If the average deal is worth Rs. 50 lakh, that's Rs. 7.5 crore in monthly revenue.

If 30% of leads get no follow-up — which is the industry average without a system — that's 150 leads lost. At 3% conversion, that's 4.5 deals. At Rs. 50 lakh each, that's **Rs. 2.25 crore in lost revenue every month**.

The CRM that prevents this costs a fraction of a single lost deal.

Now add the leads that get follow-up, but late. Research consistently shows that responding to a real estate inquiry within 5 minutes versus 30 minutes increases conversion by 4x. Without automated follow-ups, most teams respond in hours, not minutes.

## What a Purpose-Built Real Estate CRM Does

When we built our Real Estate CRM, we focused on the specific problems Indian developers face:

**Automatic portal integration.** Leads from 99acres, MagicBricks, Housing.com, Facebook Ads, and Google Ads flow in automatically. No CSV imports. No manual entry. No leads lost in transit.

**Intelligent deduplication.** When the same person inquires through 99acres and Facebook Ads, the system recognizes them as one lead — not two leads assigned to two different salespeople competing against each other.

**WhatsApp-first automation.** Automated follow-up sequences via WhatsApp Business API. Site visit reminders via WhatsApp. Post-visit follow-ups via WhatsApp. Because that's where Indian property buyers actually communicate.

**Site visit management.** Schedule visits, track attendance, record feedback, trigger post-visit sequences. The site visit is the most critical conversion point in real estate — it deserves its own workflow, not a calendar entry.

**Channel partner portal.** Brokers get their own login. They can see available inventory, submit leads, track deal progress, and see commission status. No more "call me for updates" conversations.

**RERA-compliant documentation.** Agreements, approvals, KYC documents, and disclosures are managed in a structured document vault with audit trails.

**Pipeline visibility.** At any point, the developer can see: How many leads came in this week? From which sources? How many site visits are scheduled? What's the conversion rate by salesperson? What's the projected revenue this quarter?

## The Follow-Up Rate Problem

The single most important metric in real estate lead management is the follow-up rate — the percentage of leads that receive timely, structured follow-up after initial inquiry.

Without a system, follow-up is inconsistent (and "follow-up" often means a single phone call, then nothing).

With automated sequences triggered by lead behavior and pipeline stage, teams see **dramatically higher follow-up rates** with multi-touch sequences that run for weeks.

That improvement in follow-up rate, applied across hundreds of leads per month, is the difference between a project that hits its sales targets and one that doesn't.
