## Electronics Retailer — Hyderabad

### Digi World Electronics: From IMEI Nightmares to Full Inventory Control

Rajesh Reddy runs three Digi World Electronics stores across Hyderabad — one in Ameerpet, one in Kukatpally, and one in Dilsukhnagar. Between the three locations, he carries Samsung, Vivo, Oppo, Xiaomi, OnePlus, and Realme devices. At any given time, that is roughly 2,800 active IMEIs across his network.

### The Challenge

Rajesh's team was tracking IMEIs in Excel sheets — one per store, updated manually at end of day. Here is what that looked like in practice:

- **Shrinkage was bleeding money.** During a quarterly audit, Rajesh discovered 14 devices worth ₹3.2 lakh were unaccounted for. Some were inter-store transfers never logged. Some were just missing. He had no way to know which.
- **Brand scheme payouts were slipping through the cracks.** Samsung and Vivo ran 6 overlapping schemes in a single quarter. Rajesh's staff tracked them on paper. By the time they compiled claims, two schemes had expired. Estimated loss: ₹1.4 lakh in unclaimed payouts that quarter alone.
- **The owner had no real-time visibility.** Rajesh spent his Sundays visiting each store to reconcile stock. He would call managers during the week asking "how many Redmi Note 13 Pros do we have?" and get three different answers depending on when they last updated the sheet.

### The Solution

Rajesh signed up for the 14-day RetailerOS trial after seeing a demo at a Hyderabad retailer meetup. His three stores were live within 36 hours — RetailerOS handled the data migration from his Excel sheets and Tally records.

The immediate changes:

- Every IMEI is now scanned at purchase, transfer, and sale. No device moves between stores without a tracked transfer request. The barcode scanner at each billing counter captures IMEIs automatically during checkout.
- Brand schemes from Samsung, Vivo, Oppo, and Xiaomi are loaded into RetailerOS automatically. When a qualifying sale happens, the scheme is applied at billing and the claim is logged. Expiring schemes trigger alerts 7 days before deadline.
- Rajesh checks his multi-store dashboard from his phone every morning. Stock levels, daily sales, pending transfers, and scheme claim status — all in one view, updated in real time.

### The Results

After 6 months on RetailerOS:

- **Shrinkage dropped by 92%.** Only 1 device discrepancy in 6 months, traced to a data entry error on day one — not a missing phone.
- **₹4.7 lakh in brand scheme payouts recovered** that would have been missed under the old system. That is more than 26x the annual cost of RetailerOS for all three stores.
- **Sunday reconciliation trips eliminated.** Rajesh now spends 15 minutes each morning on his dashboard instead of 5 hours every weekend at stores.
- **Staff billing speed increased by 35%** thanks to scan-to-bill with auto scheme application.

> "I was losing more money to missed schemes every quarter than RetailerOS costs me in a year. The IMEI tracking alone would have justified it — the scheme recovery was a bonus I did not expect."
> — *Rajesh R., Owner, Digi World Electronics, Hyderabad*

## Telecom Multi-Brand Retailer — Pune

### Mobile Junction: One Store, Eight Brands, Zero Missed Schemes

Priya Kulkarni owns Mobile Junction, a single-store multi-brand mobile shop in Pune's FC Road area. She stocks Samsung, Vivo, Oppo, Xiaomi, Realme, OnePlus, Motorola, and Nothing. The shop does 180–220 handset sales per month with a 4-person team.

### The Challenge

Priya's store was profitable, but she knew she was leaving money on the table — and spending too much time on work that should not require a commerce degree.

- **Eight brands, 12+ active schemes at any time.** Each brand has different scheme structures — Samsung runs cashbacks on specific models, Vivo has retailer margin boosters tied to volume targets, Oppo runs combo deals with accessories. Priya maintained a physical register and a WhatsApp group where brand reps posted scheme updates. She missed at least 2–3 scheme claims every month. At an average of ₹800–₹2,500 per missed claim, that was ₹15,000–₹20,000 walking out the door monthly.
- **GST billing was a daily headache.** Priya was using a basic billing software that did not auto-populate HSN codes for telecom products. Her accountant flagged 23 invoices with incorrect tax breakdowns in a single quarter. Fixing them took 12 hours of back-and-forth. She lived in fear of a GST notice.
- **Manual billing ate up staff time.** Each sale took 6–8 minutes — manually entering IMEI, looking up the price, checking if a scheme applied, calculating GST, printing the invoice. During Diwali season, the queue backed up out the door.

### The Solution

Priya started her RetailerOS trial on a Monday. By Wednesday, her store was fully operational on the platform — existing inventory imported, all 800+ active IMEIs catalogued, and her team trained on the billing flow.

What changed immediately:

- RetailerOS auto-loads active schemes from all eight brands. When a staff member scans a Samsung Galaxy A55 at billing, the system checks for applicable cashbacks, exchange bonuses, and combo deals — and applies them. The scheme claim is logged automatically. No register. No memory. No missed deadlines.
- GST-compliant invoicing is built into every transaction. HSN code 8517 for mobile phones, 8518 for headphones, proper CGST/SGST breakdowns, e-invoice ready. Priya's accountant now downloads quarterly reports directly from RetailerOS instead of fixing invoices.
- Billing time dropped to under 3 minutes per sale. Scan the IMEI, confirm the customer details, scheme auto-applies, payment mode selected, invoice printed. Done.

### The Results

After 4 months on RetailerOS:

- **₹1.8 lakh in additional scheme payouts claimed** — money that was previously missed due to manual tracking. That is roughly ₹45,000 per month in recovered revenue.
- **Zero GST compliance errors.** Not one invoice correction needed since switching. Priya's accountant calls RetailerOS "the best thing that happened to his practice."
- **Billing time cut from 7 minutes to 2.5 minutes per sale.** During Diwali, Priya's team handled 38 sales in a single day without a queue — a first in 5 years of operation.
- **8 hours per week freed up** that Priya previously spent on scheme tracking, invoice corrections, and manual reconciliation. She now uses that time to manage vendor relationships and plan inventory.

> "I used to track schemes in a diary and hope I did not miss any. Now RetailerOS tells me exactly what is pending, what is expiring, and what I have already claimed. Last month I claimed every single scheme payout for the first time ever."

## Fashion & Accessories Retailer — Bangalore

### TrendSet Fashion: Five Stores, One Dashboard, Complete Control

Vikram Hegde started TrendSet Fashion with a single store in Bangalore's Commercial Street in 2019. By 2025, he had expanded to 5 locations — Commercial Street, Indiranagar, Koramangala, Whitefield, and Jayanagar. His inventory includes branded fashion wear, watches, sunglasses, bags, and fashion accessories. At any time, he has ₹1.8 crore worth of stock spread across the five stores.

### The Challenge

Vikram's growth created operational problems that his existing systems — a mix of Tally and spreadsheets — could not handle.

- **Inter-store transfers were a black hole.** When the Koramangala store needed a specific watch model that Indiranagar had in stock, a staff member would WhatsApp the other store, someone would pack and send it by delivery, and maybe — maybe — someone would update both spreadsheets. In one quarter, Vikram found ₹4.6 lakh worth of "in transit" inventory that had arrived at destination stores weeks ago but was never logged. Some items showed as available in two stores simultaneously.
- **No stock visibility across locations.** Vikram could not answer a basic question: "How many units of this Ray-Ban model do we have across all stores?" without calling five store managers. By the time he got answers, the numbers were already stale. Overstocking at one location while another was sold out was a weekly occurrence.
- **Staff accountability was nonexistent.** With 22 employees across 5 stores, Vikram had no way to track who processed which sale, who initiated which transfer, or who applied which discount. When discrepancies appeared, everyone pointed fingers. He suspected a few staff members were giving unauthorized discounts to friends — but had no data to prove it.

### The Solution

Vikram's initial concern was that RetailerOS was built for telecom and electronics. After a demo call, the Khosha Systems team showed him how the platform's core capabilities — serial/SKU tracking, multi-store dashboard, role-based access, and GST billing — worked equally well for fashion and accessories retail. His 5 stores were live within one week, including migration of his full product catalogue and opening stock from Tally.

Key changes:

- Every inter-store transfer now requires a digital request, approval from the receiving store manager, and confirmation on receipt. Both stores' inventory updates automatically. No WhatsApp. No guessing. No ghost inventory.
- The multi-store dashboard gives Vikram a single view of stock levels, sales, and transfers across all 5 locations. He can drill down by store, by product category, by brand, or by individual SKU. Slow-moving stock alerts tell him when Whitefield has been sitting on ₹80,000 worth of watches for 45 days.
- Role-based access means each staff member logs in with their own credentials. Every sale, every discount, every transfer is tied to a person. Store managers can process transfers and apply standard discounts. Only Vikram can approve discounts above 15%. The analytics dashboard shows per-employee sales performance, discount patterns, and transaction volumes.

### The Results

After 5 months on RetailerOS:

- **Inter-store transfer discrepancies dropped to zero.** Every item is tracked from dispatch to receipt. The ₹4.6 lakh quarterly "mystery inventory" problem is gone.
- **Stock rebalancing across stores improved by 70%.** When a product is selling fast in Koramangala but sitting idle in Jayanagar, Vikram sees it the same day and initiates a transfer. Stockouts at individual stores dropped by 60%.
- **Unauthorized discounts eliminated.** Within the first month, RetailerOS flagged 3 staff members who had been applying 20–25% discounts not approved by management. The estimated revenue leakage was ₹35,000–₹40,000 per month. After implementing role-based discount controls, that dropped to zero.
- **₹2.1 lakh saved in the first quarter** through better stock utilization, eliminated shrinkage, and recovered margin from unauthorized discounts.
- **Vikram's weekly store visits reduced from 5 to 1.** He now does a single Saturday round across stores for team morale — not for stock checks. Everything else is on his dashboard.
