## Section 2: Problem / Solution

**Section headline:** Running a Retail Store in India Shouldn't Be This Hard

**Intro copy:**
You got into retail to sell phones and electronics — not to fight with spreadsheets, chase brand scheme payouts, or reconcile IMEI numbers at midnight. But that's where most retailers end up.

### Pain Point 1: IMEI and Serial Number Chaos

**The problem:** You track thousands of IMEIs across purchases, transfers, and sales — using Excel or paper registers. One wrong entry means a lost device, a disputed warranty, or a failed audit.

**How RetailerOS solves it:** Every IMEI is scanned and tracked from the moment stock arrives to the moment it's sold. Barcode scan, camera capture, or manual entry — one flow, zero gaps. Full audit trail per device across every store.

### Pain Point 2: Brand Scheme Leakage

**The problem:** Samsung, Vivo, Oppo, Xiaomi — every brand runs schemes with different rules, timelines, and payout structures. You're tracking them on paper or in your head. You miss claims. You lose money.

**How RetailerOS solves it:** RetailerOS automatically loads active schemes, applies them at billing, and maintains a live tally of pending claims, approved payouts, and expiring offers. No more missed cashbacks or forgotten combo deals.

### Pain Point 3: Multi-Store Blindspots

**The problem:** If you run more than one location, you're flying blind. Stock levels are guesses. Transfers aren't tracked. You find out about problems after they've cost you.

**How RetailerOS solves it:** Real-time inventory sync across every store, serial-level transfer tracking, and a centralized dashboard that shows you exactly what's happening — per store, per brand, per SKU — right now.

### Pain Point 4: GST Compliance is a Time Sink

**The problem:** Every invoice needs correct HSN codes, proper tax breakdowns, and GST-compliant formatting. Returns and credit notes add another layer of complexity. One mistake and you're dealing with notices.

**How RetailerOS solves it:** GST-compliant billing is built in — HSN codes, tax invoices, reverse charge handling, and return workflows. Your invoices are compliant by default, not by effort.

**Section headline:** RetailerOS — Frequently Asked Questions

**Intro copy:** Common questions from store owners and retail managers about RetailerOS.

### Q1: What types of retailers is RetailerOS built for?
RetailerOS is purpose-built for mobile phone shops, multi-brand electronics stores, telecom distributors, and consumer electronics retailers in India. If you sell phones, laptops, TVs, or accessories and deal with IMEI numbers, brand schemes, and GST billing — this is built for you.

### Q3: Does RetailerOS support IMEI and serial number tracking?
Yes — it's a core feature, not an add-on. IMEI and serial number capture is built into the billing flow via barcode scan, camera capture, or manual entry. Every device is tracked from purchase through sale with a full audit trail.

Yes. Multi-store support includes serial-level inventory sync, inter-store transfer tracking, centralized analytics with per-store views, and role-based access control. Owners see everything; managers see their store.

Yes. We provide assisted data migration from Tally, Gofrugal, Ginesys, spreadsheets, or any existing system. Your IMEI records, inventory data, customer information, and transaction history are imported during onboarding.

No. RetailerOS is a professional tool and we invest in properly onboarding every customer. However, we offer a fully-functional 14-day free trial — no credit card required. This gives you enough time to run your store on RetailerOS and see the difference.

### Q9: What if I need help after setup?
Every plan includes priority support via email and WhatsApp. Most issues are resolved within 2 hours during business hours. For multi-store accounts, you get a dedicated account manager.

### Q10: How is RetailerOS different from Tally or generic POS software?
Tally and generic POS systems weren't built for IMEI-based retail. They can't track individual devices, manage brand schemes, or handle telecom-specific workflows.
