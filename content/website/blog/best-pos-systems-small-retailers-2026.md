## 2. Shopify POS

**Best for:** Retailers who already sell online through Shopify and want a unified online-offline system.

Shopify POS is the natural extension of the Shopify ecommerce platform. If your business is primarily online and you are adding a physical store, Shopify POS gives you a single inventory pool across channels with minimal setup.

**Key features:** Unified online and in-store inventory, integrated payments, customer profiles with cross-channel purchase history, staff management, and hardware options including card readers and receipt printers.

**Pricing:** POS Lite is included with all Shopify plans (from $39/month). POS Pro is $89/month per location.

**Pros:** Excellent omnichannel experience, large app ecosystem, clean interface.

**Cons:** GST compliance requires third-party apps. No IMEI tracking. Pricing in USD adds exchange rate variability for Indian businesses.

## 4. Square for Retail

**Best for:** New and very small retailers who want a simple, low-cost POS to get started quickly.

## How to Choose the Right POS System for Your Store

Before committing to a platform, evaluate your options against these criteria:

**1. Industry fit.** A POS built for your retail category saves weeks of workarounds. If you sell phones, you need IMEI tracking. If you manage brand schemes, you need scheme reconciliation. General-purpose systems rarely handle these well.

**2. Compliance readiness.** For Indian retailers, GST invoicing, e-invoicing (IRN), and HSN code support are non-negotiable. If the POS does not handle these natively, expect third-party plugin costs and manual workarounds.

**3. Offline reliability.** Your POS should work during internet outages and sync cleanly when connectivity returns — critical for Tier-2 and Tier-3 locations.

**4. Total cost of ownership.** Factor in transaction fees, hardware costs, add-on charges, and third-party integration costs beyond the monthly subscription.

**5. Scalability.** Check whether the platform supports multi-store operations with centralised inventory and reporting, and what it costs to add locations.

**6. Support quality.** When billing goes down during peak hours, response time matters. Verify support availability in your time zone and language.
