## 3. Full Description (~500 words)

### The problem no one talks about

India sells over 300 million phones a year through 1.2 million+ retail outlets. Most of these stores -- from single-counter shops in tier-3 towns to multi-outlet chains -- still run on spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and generic billing software that was designed for grocery stores.

The result? IMEI numbers get mismatched. Brand scheme cashbacks (Samsung, Vivo, Oppo, Xiaomi -- each with different rules, different timelines, different portals) go unclaimed. GST filings become a monthly nightmare. Warranty claims turn into guesswork. And nobody knows what's actually in stock across locations until someone physically counts.

We've watched this for 15 years. We decided to fix it.

### What is RetailerOS?

RetailerOS is the operating system for telecom and electronics retail in India. Not a generic POS with a telecom skin. Not an ERP crammed into a tablet. It's software built from the ground up for the way Indian phone retailers actually work.

**IMEI & Serial Number Tracking** -- Every device tracked from purchase to sale to warranty. No more IMEI mismatches, no more lost units, no more audit anxiety.

**Scheme & Offer Engine** -- Automatically maps brand cashback schemes from Samsung, Vivo, Oppo, Xiaomi, and others. Tracks eligibility, calculates payouts, flags expiring claims. Retailers using RetailerOS recover 10-15% more scheme money they were previously leaving on the table.

**GST-Compliant Billing** -- Generate invoices that are actually compliant. HSN codes, proper tax breakdowns, return handling -- all baked in, not bolted on.

**Multi-Store Sync** -- Real-time inventory, sales, and performance data across all your locations. Transfer stock between stores without losing track.

**Brand & Distributor Portal** -- Give your brand reps and distributors controlled access to sell-through data, scheme compliance, and stock visibility.

### The results speak

Retailers on RetailerOS see faster checkout through IMEI auto-capture, improved inventory accuracy through serial-number tracking, significant reduction in manual work, lower shrinkage, and more scheme payouts recovered.

### Who built this?

Khosha Systems, Bangalore. Our founder Ankit Mehta has spent 15+ years in Indian retail technology, watching the gap between what telecom retailers need and what software gives them. RetailerOS is the product of obsessively studying how these stores actually operate -- the chaos, the workarounds, the money left on the table -- and building software that fits.

We're not trying to be everything for everyone. We're trying to be exactly right for Indian telecom and electronics retail.

## 5. Maker Story (About Section, 300-400 words)

### Why We Built RetailerOS

Fifteen years ago, I walked into a telecom retail store in Bangalore to help set up their billing system. The owner -- a sharp businessman running three locations and doing serious revenue -- was tracking his Samsung and Vivo scheme cashbacks in a paper diary. He had missed claims worth over 4 lakhs the previous quarter. Not because he was careless, but because no software he could buy understood how brand schemes work in Indian telecom retail.

That moment stuck with me.

Over the next decade, I worked across retail technology in India -- POS systems, inventory management, ERP implementations. And I kept seeing the same pattern: the software available to telecom and electronics retailers was either generic billing tools that didn't understand IMEI tracking, or enterprise ERPs that cost more than the store's annual profit.

India's telecom retail market is enormous -- 1.2 million outlets, 300 million phones sold every year, a complex web of brands, distributors, schemes, and regulations. Yet the software serving this market was built for someone else and awkwardly retrofitted.

In 2010, we founded Khosha Systems with one belief: Indian telecom retailers deserve software built specifically for them. Not adapted, not configured, not customized -- built.

We spent months inside retail stores. We mapped every workflow. We understood the scheme structures of every major brand. We learned why retailers keep parallel records, why GST filing day is dreaded, why multi-store owners can never trust their stock numbers.

Then we built RetailerOS.

Every feature exists because a real retailer needed it. IMEI tracking isn't a module we added -- it's in the DNA. The scheme engine isn't a generic discount tool -- it maps actual Samsung, Vivo, Oppo, and Xiaomi cashback structures. GST compliance isn't a checkbox -- it's woven into every transaction.

Today, retailers on RetailerOS check out customers faster, maintain better inventory accuracy, and recover more brand scheme payouts they were previously losing.

We're a small, focused team in Bangalore. We're not trying to build software for every retailer in the world. We're trying to build the best possible software for Indian telecom and electronics retail. That's our mission. That's all we do.
