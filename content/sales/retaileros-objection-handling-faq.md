## Switching & Migration

### "We already use Tally. Why do we need something else?"

Tally is an accounting tool. It was never designed for retail operations. Tally cannot track an IMEI from purchase to sale, auto-apply a Samsung scheme at billing, sync inventory across stores in real time, or forecast which SKUs to reorder. RetailerOS was built specifically for telecom and electronics retail in India — IMEI tracking, scheme management, GST billing, multi-store ops. Tally handles your books. RetailerOS runs your business.

**The difference:** Tally tells you what happened last month. RetailerOS tells you what to do tomorrow.

### "How is this different from Shopify POS or Square?"

Shopify POS and Square were built for Western retail — coffee shops in Brooklyn, boutiques in London. They have no IMEI tracking, no Indian brand scheme management, no native GST compliance, and no understanding of how Indian retail actually works. Shopify's POS Pro costs $89/location/month on top of their base plan for features that RetailerOS includes by default. RetailerOS was built for Indian retail from day one — GST, brand schemes, IMEI workflows, UPI, WhatsApp notifications, and Hindi support. All included at ₹1,999/month.

### "What about iQmetrix? They specialise in telecom retail."

iQmetrix is built for North American carriers — T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T. They have no GST support, no Indian brand scheme management, and no presence in India. Their architecture splits into six separate modules with opaque enterprise pricing and months-long deployments. RetailerOS gives you one unified platform, transparent pricing (₹1,999/month), and a 48-hour go-live. They built for T-Mobile stores in Texas. We built for mobile stores in Tilak Nagar.

### "What about Gofrugal or Ginesys?"

Gofrugal and Ginesys are built for FMCG and general retail — groceries, supermarkets, apparel. They treat electronics as an afterthought. Neither has native IMEI-level tracking, automated brand scheme management for Samsung/Vivo/Oppo, or telecom-specific workflows. RetailerOS was designed from the ground up for serialised inventory retail. If you sell devices with IMEI numbers, you need software that understands IMEI numbers — not software that was built for barcoded grocery items and adapted for electronics.
